# Product brief

## One-line pitch

Somm Atlas is an interactive map connecting appellation law, geology, wine
chemistry, and sensory perception.

## Initial layers

1. **Place** — appellation boundaries, geology, soil, altitude, and exposure.
2. **Aroma chemistry** — compound families, representative molecules, sensory
   markers, and evidence strength.
3. **Phenolic architecture** — tannin and anthocyanin profiles, extraction, and
   pigment evolution.
4. **Palate architecture** — acidity, alcohol, body, tannin, texture, bitterness,
   and finish.
5. **Appellation rules** — grapes, percentages, geography, yield, planting,
   maturation, labelling, and special mentions.

## Core interactions

- Select a compound family and see associated regional and varietal clusters.
- Select a geological formation and compare wines grown on related substrates.
- Compare the sensory and chemical fingerprints of two or three wine archetypes.
- Inspect the official rules that make a wine eligible for an appellation.
- Answer “Could this be…?” classification questions with cited explanations.

## Data standard

Every substantive claim should include:

- scope: appellation, subzone, grape, producer, cuvée, or vintage;
- classification: legal requirement, measured fact, published association,
  common practice, historical practice, or interpretation;
- source URL and document date;
- original quotation or source fragment where licensing permits;
- confidence level; and
- last verification date.
